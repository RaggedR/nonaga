We tried 12 approaches to make a neural network play Nonaga — curriculum learning, draw shaping, endgame bootstrap, MCTS sign fix, self-play loop, policy bootstrap, island-model GA, island-model AlphaZero with cross-play, GA imitation training, and GA self-play distillation. The island-model GA found a 14-weight evaluation function in 3.5 hours that beats every NN we trained. The best NN (island 2, iteration 20, 570K parameters, ~30 hours of training) managed 50/50. The fundamental problem is twofold: Nonaga's strategic space fits in 14 numbers, and equal opponents draw forever — blocking both self-play and distillation. The GA wins because it searches the right space (14 feature weights) instead of the wrong one (570K neural network parameters from raw board pixels).
